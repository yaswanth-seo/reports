Himalayan salt tiles (or bricks) have become a massive trend in interior design, moving from high-end spas into residential homes. They offer a unique, organic aesthetic that brings warmth and texture to a space.
If you’re considering a salt wall, here is a breakdown of what you need to know regarding installation, benefits, and maintenance.
### Why Use Salt Tiles?
* **Aesthetics:** When backlit, the tiles glow with a warm, amber hue. Each tile is naturally unique, offering variations in pink, white, and deep orange. * **Ambient Lighting:** They are frequently used to create "salt rooms" or feature walls in bedrooms and meditation spaces to promote a calming atmosphere. * **Durability:** In dry environments, salt is incredibly hard and stable. It won't degrade over time if kept away from moisture.
### Installation Methods
There are two primary ways to get these onto your wall:

### Critical Considerations
Before you start sticking rocks to your walls, keep these "physics of salt" in mind:
* **Weight:** Himalayan salt is heavy. A standard 8"x4"x1" tile weighs about 2.5 lbs (1.1 kg). Ensure your wall studs and sub-structure can handle the cumulative load. * **The "Melting" Factor:** Salt is **hygroscopic**, meaning it attracts water from the air. * * **Do not** install salt tiles in bathrooms, kitchens, or outdoors. * In high-humidity areas, the salt may "sweat," leading to puddles at the base of the wall and potential structural damage. * **Backlighting:** If you want that classic glow, use **LED strips**. Traditional bulbs can generate too much localized heat, which might cause the salt to crack or expand unevenly.
### Maintenance
Maintenance is surprisingly low. Since salt is naturally antimicrobial, you don't need to scrub it. A simple **dry microfiber cloth** or a light vacuum with a brush attachment is usually all you need to remove dust. Never use wet cleaners or sprays.
