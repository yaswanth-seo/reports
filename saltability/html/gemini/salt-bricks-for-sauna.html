Using salt bricks in a sauna—often referred to as a **Himalayan Salt Sauna**—is a fantastic way to level up your relaxation game. Beyond just looking like a high-end spa, adding salt elements can actually change the "feel" of the air.

## Why Use Salt Bricks?
The primary appeal is **halotherapy** (salt therapy). When the bricks are heated, they release negative ions into the air.
* **Respiratory Support:** Breathable salt particles can help clear airways and reduce inflammation, which is why people with allergies or asthma often swear by them. * **Skin Health:** Salt is naturally antibacterial and antimicrobial, which can help detoxify the skin. * **Ambiance:** Himalayan salt has a natural pinkish-orange hue. When backlit with LED strips, it creates a warm, grounding glow that’s much more relaxing than standard sauna lighting.
## Installation & Best Practices
You don't necessarily need to build an entire wall out of salt to get the benefits. Here are the common ways to integrate them:
### 1. The Salt Wall
The most popular method. You stack the bricks (usually $8 \times 4 \times 1$ inches) against an existing wall.
* **Adhesive:** Use a specialized non-toxic, heat-resistant salt glue. * **Lighting:** Leave a small gap (about 1–2 inches) between the salt wall and the sauna wall to install heat-resistant LED backlighting.
### 2. The Salt Frame
If a full wall is too much work or too expensive, you can build a smaller wooden frame filled with salt bricks and mount it like a piece of "living" art.
### 3. Near the Heater
Placing a few bricks or a bowl of salt stones near (but usually not directly *on*) the heater helps maximize the release of ions, as higher temperatures accelerate the process.
