A detox salt bath is a classic way to unwind, soothe sore muscles, and give your skin a little extra attention. While the term "detox" is often used loosely—your liver and kidneys handle the heavy lifting of actual detoxification—soaking in mineral-rich water is fantastic for relaxation and circulation.
Here is how to put together an effective soak:

## Instructions for the Perfect Soak
1. **Hydrate First:** Drink a full glass of water before getting in. The warm water can cause you to sweat, and you want to stay hydrated. 2. **Temperature Matters:** Aim for warm, not scalding. Water that is too hot can stress the heart and overly dry out your skin. 3. **The Dissolve:** Add your salts while the tub is filling to ensure they dissolve completely. 4. **The Soak:** Stay in for **15–20 minutes**. This is the "sweet spot" for mineral absorption without pruning your skin too much. 5. **The Rinse:** Briefly rinse off in a cool shower afterward to remove any excess salt or sweat from your skin.
### Pro-Tips & Safety
* **Carrier Oils:** If using essential oils, mix them into a tablespoon of coconut or olive oil before adding them to the water. This prevents the concentrated oil from floating on top and irritating your skin. * **Safety Check:** If you have low blood pressure, diabetes, or are pregnant, consult a healthcare provider before taking long, hot baths. * **Post-Bath Care:** Your skin will be primed to absorb moisture, so apply a good lotion or body oil immediately after drying off.
> **Note:** If you experience dizziness or a rapid heartbeat while soaking, carefully exit the tub immediately and cool down.
