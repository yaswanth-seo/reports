Integrating halotherapy (salt therapy) into a spa can range from adding a portable device to a treatment room to building a full-scale "salt cave."[www.saltchamberinc.com](https://www.saltchamberinc.com/blog/spa-treatments-to-pair-with-halotherapy-treatment#:~:text=In%20some%20cases%2C%20practitioners%20even,capitalize%20on%20the%20therapy's%20benefits.) The goal is to create an environment where a **halogenerator** can effectively disperse medical-grade salt aerosol.
Here is a breakdown of the essential equipment and considerations for a professional spa setup.
## 1. The Core Technology: Halogenerators
The halogenerator is the only piece of equipment that makes a room "halotherapy-capable." It crushes pharmaceutical-grade salt into micro-particles (0.5 to 5 microns) and blows them into the air.[www.saltchamberinc.com](https://www.saltchamberinc.com/resources/what-is-a-halogenerator#:~:text=A%20reliable%20halogenerator%20releases%20that,particle%20sizes%20and%20concentration%20levels.)
* **Wall-Mounted Units:** Best for dedicated salt rooms. They are programmed for specific session lengths and concentrations.[vivasalt-halogenerator.eu](https://vivasalt-halogenerator.eu/halogenerator/) * **Portable/Compact Units:** Ideal for retrofitting existing massage rooms or for use in "salt booths" and "salt cocoons."[www.saltchamberinc.com](https://www.saltchamberinc.com/blog/how-to-choose-best-halotherapy-machine) * **Smart Features:** Look for units with Bluetooth or Wi-Fi control, allowing therapists to adjust settings via a tablet.[halogenerators-saltrooms-inhalators-for.eu](https://halogenerators-saltrooms-inhalators-for.eu/halogenerators-prizma/halogenerator-prizsalt-with-touch-screen/)

## 3. Structural & Functional Essentials
If you are building a dedicated room, you need more than just the machine:
* **Ventilation & HVAC:** You need a dedicated exhaust system to pull the salt air out after a session so it doesn't corrode the rest of your spa's electronics or ductwork.[www.saltchamberinc.com](https://www.saltchamberinc.com/blog/dos-and-donts-adding-salt-therapy-to-your-business) * **Salt Decor:** While "Himalayan salt bricks" look beautiful and provide ambient negative ions, they **do not** provide the respiratory benefits of a halogenerator. They are used primarily for aesthetics and mood lighting. * **Non-Corrosive Materials:** Salt is corrosive.[wilkinsoncoutts.com](https://wilkinsoncoutts.com/salt-is-bad-for-your-health-corrosion-basics/#:~:text=Basics%20Part%201-,Salt%20is%20bad%20for%20your%20health!%20%E2%80%93,to%20%E2%80%9Ceat-away%E2%80%9D.) Use plastic, wood, or specialized salt-resistant coatings for furniture and fixtures.
## 4. Consumables
* **Pharmaceutical Grade Sodium Chloride (NaCl):** You cannot use table salt or sea salt in a halogenerator. It must be 99.99% pure medical-grade salt to ensure it is free of minerals and impurities that could irritate the lungs.[www.saltchamberinc.com](https://www.saltchamberinc.com/blog/types-of-salt-explained)
### Key Technical Consideration
> **Note:** Proper humidity control is vital. If a salt room is too humid (above 40-50%), the salt particles will clump together and fall to the ground rather than remaining suspended in the air for inhalation.
