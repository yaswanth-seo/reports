### How to Spot a "Fake" Lamp
As these lamps have stayed popular, many imitation "salt-styled" plastic or glass lamps have entered the market. Use this checklist to ensure you're getting real Himalayan rock salt:
* **The "Sweat" Test:** Real salt is **hygroscopic** (it attracts moisture). In humid conditions, a real lamp will "sweat" or feel damp. If it stays perfectly dry in a humid room, it’s likely fake. * **Fragility:** Real salt lamps are very brittle. If you drop it and it doesn't chip, or if it arrived in the mail without any salt dust in the box, it might be molded plastic. * **Light Pattern:** Authentic lamps emit a **muted, uneven glow**. If the light is uniform, extremely bright (like a standard light bulb), or perfectly white (unless it’s a rare, expensive white salt variety), proceed with caution. * **Origin:** Genuine Himalayan salt is almost exclusively mined from the **Punjab region of Pakistan**. Check the label for its origin.
> **Pro Tip:** Always place your lamp on a coaster or decorative plate. Because real lamps "breathe" moisture, they can occasionally leak salty water which might damage wooden furniture.
