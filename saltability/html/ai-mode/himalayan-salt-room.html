A Himalayan salt room (or salt cave) is `a therapeutic, spa-like environment where the walls and floors are lined with pink Himalayan salt blocks and crystals` . These rooms are designed to mimic the microclimate of natural salt mines and are used for **halotherapy** (salt therapy) to improve respiratory health, skin conditions, and mental well-being.[[1]](https://osthoff.com/salt-room/)[[2]](https://sparoyale.net/blog/himalayan-salt-sauna-benefits/#:~:text=Spas%20can%20offer%20various%20spa,potassium%2C%20magnesium%2C%20and%20calcium.)[[3]](https://health.clevelandclinic.org/salt-cave-benefits#:~:text=Types%20of%20salt%20therapy,evidence%20to%20support%20the%20claims.)[[4]](https://sparoyale.net/blog/halotherapy-benefits-and-what-to-expect/#:~:text=What%20Is%20Halotherapy?,the%20respiratory%20system%20and%20skin.) Types of Salt Rooms - **Active Salt Rooms** : These use a **halogenerator** , a machine that grinds pharmaceutical-grade salt into microscopic particles (1–5 microns) and disperses them into the air. This is considered the most effective method for therapeutic inhalation. - **Passive Salt Rooms** : These rely on the natural presence of large quantities of salt (bricks or lamps) without a machine. While highly relaxing and meditative, they provide a much lower concentration of airborne salt particles.[[1]](https://www.webmd.com/balance/what-is-halotherapy#:~:text=Halotherapy%20can%20also%20be%20used,can%20be%20used%20to%20treat:)[[2]](https://www.amtamassage.org/about/news/halotherapy/#:~:text=What%20Is%20Halotherapy?,minutes%20up%20to%2045%20minutes.)[[3]](https://www.opalcollection.com/blog/what-is-halotherapy/#:~:text=HALOGENERATOR:%20THE%20MAGIC%20IN%20THE,and%20disperses%20throughout%20the%20room.)[[4]](https://www.saltchamberinc.com/blog/are-salt-rooms-safe#:~:text=These%20rooms%20are%20specifically%20designed,remains%20static%20in%20the%20room.)
